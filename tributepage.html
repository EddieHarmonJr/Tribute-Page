Dr. Mae Jemison

The 1st African-American Woman In Space

    Here's 10 Interesting Facts About Mae Jemison:

  • 👶🏾 1956 - Mae Jemison is born in Decatur, AL. Yet, she would call Chicago, IL home when she moved there 3 years later.
  • 🎓 1973 - Graduated from Morgan Park High School, Chicago, Illinois
  • 🔬 1977 - Received a Bachelor of Science degree in chemical engineering from the Stanford University. Not only did she attend Stanford on a National Acheivement Scholarship, she double majored in chemical engineering and in African and Afro-American Studies. If those facts alone weren't enough, she was admitted to Stanford when she was 16!
  • 🇸🇱 1981 - After Jemison obtained her M.D. in 1981 from Cornell University Medical College, she interned at Los Angeles County/University of Southern California Medical Center and later worked as a general practitioner. For the next two and a half years, she was the area Peace Corps medical officer for Sierra Leone and Liberia where she also taught and did medical research.
  • 🛰 1987 - On June 4th, Mae C. Jemison became the first African-American woman to be admitted into the NASA astronaut training program.
  • 🚀 1992 - Mae became the first African-American woman in space when she flew on the Endeavor Mission with six other astronauts. During her space mission aboard the Endeavor, she orbited Earth 126 times! The Mae C. Jemison Academy, an alternative public school in Detroit, Michigan, was named after her.
  • 📺 1993 - She's the first real astronaut to appear on Star Trek: The Next Generation.
  • 👩🏾‍🏫 1993 - Mae retired from NASA and became a teacher at Dartmouth. She also started the Jemison Group, an organization that does research in technology.
  • 🚓 1996 - Despite all of the amazing accomplishments listed above (which represents only a brief selection), Mae was still a victim of police brutality after a traffic stop encounter in which she afterwards took legal action and sued following the incident.
  • ❹ Did You Know - In addition to English, Mae is fluent Russian, Japanese, and Swahili! She's quadrilingual!
